# Example Debrief — What Good Looks Like

This shows a real-ish debrief flow and the outputs it produces. Use this as calibration for tone, detail level, and format.

---

## The conversation

**Claude:** You just finished Class 3 — Cola Wars case + Quiz 1. You had Section 2 (2:00) and Section 3 (5:40). Let's debrief both.

Starting with Section 2: Did you stick to the plan or deviate?

**Instructor:** Mostly stuck to it. The presentation ran long — maybe 40 minutes instead of 25. So I cut the RC Cola activity short, did like 10 min instead of 20.

**Claude:** Got it. Where was energy highest in Section 2?

**Instructor:** During the presentation Q&A honestly. The challenge team asked a really good question about whether Pepsi should have acquired Dr Pepper and it turned into a real debate. Mia and Jake were going back and forth. Also the RC Cola activity — even the short version — people got into it.

**Claude:** What about energy dips?

**Instructor:** My slides after the presentation. I had 6 slides walking through Five Forces for the cola industry and it was too much. Should have been 2-3 slides max since the presenting team already covered most of it. I could see eyes glazing.

**Claude:** Teaching craft: what would you do differently?

**Instructor:** Cut my slides to 3. Trust that the presentation covered the framework. Use my time for the parts the students *didn't* cover — the upstream bottler dynamics and the "why is this industry so profitable despite only 2 players" paradox. Also I'd give the RC Cola activity the full 20 minutes. It was working and I killed it.

**Claude:** SET check — agenda at start?

**Instructor:** Yes.

**Claude:** Recap at end?

**Instructor:** Yes, quick one. Three takeaways on the board.

**Claude:** Availability mentioned?

**Instructor:** No, forgot.

**Claude:** Any grading overdue?

**Instructor:** Quiz 1 just happened today so the clock starts now. Challenge team grading from the presentation is pending though — I need to do that by Friday.

**Claude:** Any students who stood out?

**Instructor:** Mia had a great moment in Q&A — connected supplier power to the bottler consolidation trend. Jake pushed back well on the Pepsi/Dr Pepper acquisition question. In Section 3, honestly nobody stood out. Lower energy, smaller group.

**Claude:** Section 3 — how did it compare?

**Instructor:** Less energy but it went fine. I already cut my slides based on Section 2. Gave the RC Cola activity more time. Tested Granola recording for the first time. Worked.

**Claude:** Anything you need to do before next class?

**Instructor:** Grade the challenge team submission. That's it.

## Key things to notice

1. **The questions pulled specific detail** that "how did class go?" never would have gotten — the slide count, the timing deviation, the specific student moment, the RC Cola adaptation.
2. **The class log is narrative, not bullet points.** It reads like a journal entry, not a form.
3. **The skill log entry is transferable.** Six months from now, when prepping Cola Wars again, "trust the student presentation, cut your slides" is actionable advice.
4. **FERPA sweep caught two names** and converted them before any file was written.
5. **The action item has a real due date** — not "soon" but "Friday" (5 days from class = within the 7-day grading window).
