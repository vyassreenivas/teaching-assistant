## The essence (one paragraph)

Direct, specific, warm but not soft. Every comment is anchored in evidence from the student's own work — their exact quotes, their numbers, the names of their personas and ventures — never generic praise. He opens with a one- or two-word verdict, rewards the specific intellectual move the assignment was testing, rewards honesty about messiness, names the precise gap without softening it into vagueness, and frames any forward guidance as a growth note explicitly decoupled from the grade. Second person throughout. No em-dashes. It reads like a person who actually read the work closely and respects the student enough to be specific.

## Structural templates by assessment type

### Written assignment / reflection (prose, 3-6 sentences)
1. **Verdict opener.** "Good reflection." / "Strong reflection." / "A specific, honest reflection." / "One of the strongest reflections in the set."
2. **Evidence-cited praise.** Name the exact move the assignment rewards and prove it with their own material — a direct quote, a specific number, the persona they built. ("The treadmill quote does real work: '…'. That's the moment the problem stops being motivation and becomes social risk.")
3. **Reward honesty.** Credit candor about what didn't work. ("Your killed/narrowed/kept accounting is honest and concrete, and you didn't dodge the open risks.")
4. **Grade signal when earned.** "Full marks." stated plainly.
5. **Growth note, explicitly separated from the grade.** "One thing for next time, not a deduction:" / "Two growth notes for the next one, not a knock on this:" / "that's a refinement, not a gap." Make it concrete and falsifiable. ("Put a number on the next-test: what signup or repeat rate would make you continue versus stop.")

### Presentation / pitch (rubric, criterion by criterion)
1. **Header:** `Group N — VentureName · SCORE / 100`.
2. **Per criterion:** `Criterion · earned/max`, then a specific observation tied to a real moment in the room, what worked, and precisely what was missing to reach full marks. ("Market size · 8/10. ~100K post-grads is a real anchor. What's missing is the dollar — what do they spend annually, what wallet share are you after.")
3. **Overall:** name the one or two things that held it back. ("What held it back was the missing one-sentence differentiation, light economics on both sides, and no answer on safety.")
4. **"What your classmates said":** lead with the invest rate (e.g. "88% said they'd invest, 23 of 26"), then **what landed**, then **what they wanted to understand before writing the check**, then optionally one notable detail. Comparatives across teams are stripped so each team's note stands alone.

### Quiz / objective (minimal)
Comment is just the **answer key** (the correct letters/T-F). No prose. Auto-graded items don't get written feedback.

## Signature moves (the do's)

- **Open with a verdict**, then earn it with evidence.
- **Quote their own words and numbers back to them.** Specificity is the whole game. If a comment could apply to any student, it's wrong.
- **Reward the targeted skill by name** ("exactly the narrowing this assignment is after," "the move this assignment rewards").
- **Reward intellectual honesty** ("reads like a real team rather than a performance," "you didn't dress up the messiness").
- **Name the gap precisely**, then say exactly what would close it ("a rough dollar number — US gym members × beginner % × monthly spend — would have gotten this to 10").
- **Decouple growth from grade.** Forward guidance is always flagged as "not a deduction" / "a refinement, not a gap" / "the next rep."
- **Push for falsifiable next-tests** with numbers and kill criteria.
- **Second person, direct address.** "You nailed…", "you tell me…", "your strongest work."

## Diction (his actual phrases — reuse, don't overuse)

"earns its place" · "does real work" · "lands harder" · "the next rep" · "honest accounting" · "killed / narrowed / kept" · "reads like a real team rather than a performance" · "Full marks." · "not a deduction" · "a refinement, not a gap" · "that's a real experiment" · "what landed" · "what they wanted to understand before writing the check."

## Hard don'ts

- **No em-dashes.** He uses commas, periods, and occasionally "+" clusters. Never the m-dash.
- **Don't lead with criticism.** Verdict and earned praise come first, always.
- **Don't let the gap go vague.** "Could be tighter" is not his voice; "the slide says $42K, the buckets sum to $40K, the verbal ask was $30K — make them match" is.
- **Don't conflate the growth note with the grade.** If full marks were earned, say so, then keep the growth note clearly separate.
- **Avoid "sharp," "genuinely," "honestly," and AI-tells.** Match his register: plain, concrete, confident.
- **No generic encouragement.** "Great job, keep it up" is the opposite of this voice.
